About


My name is Joshua Cruz Cintrón, I was born and raised in Puerto Rico. I am a Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) Undergraduate Student at University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez. I enjoy reading philosophy, writing poetry, playing video games, and building software. My goal is to work on small, cross-functional, and interdisciplinary teams to create efficient tools that will help solve problems across multiple domains. I am fascinated by the idea of Agile, and hope to help create and improve agile teams everywhere I set foot. My professional interests are in web development, cloud computing, DevOps, and automation. During my college career I seek the opportunity to learn about these concepts from working on my own projects, and by interning on some of the companies who are most adept at these technologies. All this with the expectation of bringing these technologies to the many teams of developers in Puerto Rico.

Poetry

Ship in a Bottle
Life's a ship in a bottle,
liberty, a galleon trapped whole
navigating a full throttle,
trying to escape through a small hole,
and with every moment's pass,
the ship remains in its hold
for although the bottle is of glass,
the captain was never told.
-J. Cruz
Early Mustn't Be
When word be spoken
with idle technique,
and dawn awoken
with the answers we seek,
the night will fall
with little to keep,
and the nightmares will call,
to sway us to sleep,
for the knowledge of all
early mustn't be.
-J. Cruz
Pride, Maize to the Crow
An improper thought,
that which we believe to know,
to ignore a freedom which others fought.
to obtain what cannot be owned.
The future that they sought,
a victory acquired slow,
attributes left to rot.
to which love is left to show.
While we work, not,
the ground is left to plow,
a white dove has been shot,
by a murderous black crow,
for a glimpse of our pride it has caught,
and a glipse of madness it has shown.
-J. Cruz
